# Systematic literature search: novel toxin syndromes for global HAB review

**Seven novel or emerging marine toxin syndromes were systematically searched**, yielding **80+ key references** and **40+ catalogable events** spanning azaspiracid shellfish poisoning, yessotoxins, cyclic imines, pectenotoxins, palytoxin/ovatoxin (Ostreopsis), Karlodinium ichthyotoxins, and notable DSP events. The search confirms critical data gaps — particularly for Ostreopsis events outside Italy, Chilean YTX closures, and Karlodinium kills in Chesapeake Bay — that can now be filled. Azaspiracid poisoning and DSP dominate the confirmed human illness record (>200 AZP cases; >20,000 DSP cases globally), while yessotoxins and pectenotoxins have caused major economic losses without confirmed human toxicity.

## 4. Pectenotoxins (PTX)

### 4.1 Key events and regulatory status

**No event globally where PTX was the sole primary regulatory concern has been identified.** PTX always co-occurs with OA-group toxins in *Dinophysis* blooms. The EU deregulated PTXs in 2021 (Delegated Regulation 2021/1374). No confirmed human illness from PTXs alone. PTX-2 showed no oral toxicity at 5,000 µg/kg in mice (Miles et al. 2004).

The **2021 French Basque coast event** (674 cases) represents the largest single Ostreopsis health incident documented outside Italy. This was also the **first report of *O. cf. ovata* on the French Atlantic coast**, demonstrating range expansion beyond the Mediterranean. The Croatian event (Pfannkuchen et al. 2012) is critically important as it shows toxic blooms can occur completely undetected by public health surveillance.

## Cross-cutting synthesis and critical notes

Three findings warrant special attention for the review. First, **azaspiracids and palytoxin/ovatoxins represent the most significant emerging human health threats**: AZP has caused illness on at least 6 occasions across 5 countries, while the 2021 French Basque coast Ostreopsis event (674 cases) demonstrates that palytoxin syndromes are expanding geographically beyond the Mediterranean. Second, **yessotoxins and pectenotoxins cause disproportionate economic damage relative to their actual toxicological risk** — no human illness has been confirmed for either toxin group, yet YTX closures plague major shellfish industries in Italy, Norway, and Chile, and PTX regulation was only relaxed by the EU in 2021. Third, **cyclic imines (spirolides, gymnodimines) remain the least understood group** with no confirmed human poisoning and no regulation, yet they are globally distributed and increasing in detection frequency as LC-MS/MS replaces the mouse bioassay.

For the Faroe Islands DSP events specifically requested, no large-scale human illness outbreak was documented in the literature, but *Dinophysis acuta* strains isolated from Faroese waters show among the highest toxin contents recorded globally (**134 pg PTX2 + 34 pg OA + 78 pg DTX-1b per cell**; Reguera et al. 2014), indicating persistent risk to the islands' small mussel aquaculture sector.

The Morocco "2008 Agadir" AZP event referenced in the search brief could not be confirmed as a discrete human poisoning incident. The key Morocco AZP references document monitoring detections from 2004–2006 (Taleb et al. 2006; Elgarch et al. 2008), with AZA2 as the dominant analogue — a distinctive profile differing from European (AZA1-dominant) contamination.
